📘 Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) Registration in India – Secure & Flexible for Your Business

Starting or expanding your business becomes easier when you choose the right structure. A Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) is designed for professionals, startups, and growing enterprises who want the flexibility of a partnership with the legal protection of a company.

  • A separate legal identity
  • Limited liability – protects your personal assets
  • Low compliance, high flexibility

👉 In short, it’s your business, your security, your ease.

🔹 Why Register an LLP for Your Business?

  1. Limited Liability – Your personal assets stay safe.
  2. Better Taxation – No dividend distribution tax, single-level taxation.
  3. Lower Compliance Burden – Fewer filings compared to companies.
  4. Flexible Capital – No minimum capital required.
  5. Perpetual Continuity – Business continues despite partner changes.
  6. Professional Credibility – Enhances trust with clients, banks, and vendors.
  7. Easy Transfer of Ownership – Simple partner changes possible.
  8. Unlimited Scalability – No maximum partner limit.

🔹 Governing Laws (Handled by Us, So You Stay Stress-Free)

  • LLP Act, 2008 & LLP Rules, 2009 – Main regulations.
  • Companies Act, 2013 – For company-to-LLP conversion.
  • FEMA, 1999 – For LLPs with foreign investment.
  • Income Tax Act, 1961 – For taxation compliance.

👉 We manage the legalities, you manage your business.

🏢 Types of LLPs in India

  1. Domestic LLP – Indian partners running businesses in India.
  2. Foreign LLP – LLPs with foreign partners or foreign LLPs setting up in India.
  3. Professional LLP – For CAs, lawyers, architects, CS, etc.
  4. Startup LLP – For ventures under Startup India scheme.
  5. Small LLP – For low-turnover, small capital contribution businesses.
  6. Conversion to LLP – Partnership firms/companies converting into LLPs.
  7. One Person LLP – Single-member LLPs (special cases).

🎯 Eligibility Criteria

  • ✔ Minimum 2 Partners (individuals or companies).
  • ✔ At least 2 Designated Partners (1 must be resident in India).
  • ✔ No maximum partner limit.
  • ✔ Minimum age: 18 years.

Every designated partner must have: DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) and DIN/DPIN (Designated Partner Identification Number).

📑 Documents Required

For Partners & Designated Partners

  • PAN Card & Passport-size photos
  • Identity proof (Aadhar/Passport/Voter ID)
  • Address proof (Utility bill / Bank statement)
  • DSCs & DINs

For Business Address

  • Utility bill / Property tax receipt
  • NOC from landlord (if rented)
  • Rent/lease agreement

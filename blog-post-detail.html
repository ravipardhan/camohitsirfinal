Understanding GST Filing Deadlines for FY 2024-25

Published on March 20, 2024 by M S Sharma & Co.

The Importance of Timely GST Filing

Staying compliant with Goods and Services Tax (GST) regulations is not just a legal requirement but a cornerstone of good financial health for any business in India. Timely filing of GST returns helps in avoiding hefty penalties, ensures a smooth flow of input tax credit (ITC), and improves your business's compliance rating. This guide will walk you through the essential deadlines for the financial year 2024-25.

"Compliance is not an expense, it's an investment in your business's future. Missing a GST deadline can have a cascading effect on your finances and reputation."

Key Monthly and Quarterly Deadlines

For most businesses, GST returns are filed on a monthly or quarterly basis, depending on their turnover and the scheme they have opted for.

  • GSTR-3B: This is a monthly summary return that all regular taxpayers need to file. The due date is typically the 20th of the following month. For those under the QRMP (Quarterly Return Monthly Payment) scheme, it's filed quarterly.
  • GSTR-1: This return contains the details of all outward supplies (sales). For monthly filers, the due date is the 11th of the next month. For QRMP filers, it's the 13th of the month following the quarter.
  • QRMP Scheme: Businesses with an aggregate turnover of up to ₹5 crores can opt for this scheme, allowing them to file returns quarterly while paying tax monthly.

Annual Return (GSTR-9)

The GSTR-9 is an annual return to be filed once a year by registered taxpayers. It is a consolidation of all the monthly/quarterly returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-2A, GSTR-3B) filed in that year. The due date for filing GSTR-9 for a financial year is the 31st of December of the subsequent year.
